For internationally trained dentists navigating the competitive process of applying to U.S. Advanced Standing Programs (ASPs), finding ways to stand out is essential. One increasingly strategic option is obtaining a **Florida dental hygienist license**.

Unlike most states, **Florida allows foreign-trained dentists to become licensed dental hygienists without completing a U.S.-based hygiene program**, as long as specific eligibility requirements are met.

This route provides a **legal opportunity to practice clinically in the U.S.**, demonstrates your **commitment to the profession**, and offers valuable **hands-on experience** that strengthens your dental school application.

---

### Why Many ASP Applicants Choose the Florida Path

Here’s why so many international dentists pursue Florida licensure before or during their dental school application process:

- **Gain U.S. Clinical Experience:** Schools value applicants with firsthand experience in American dental settings. Working as a hygienist helps you understand local patient care standards, infection control, and communication practices.
- **Earn Income While Waiting:** Instead of pausing your career, you can work and earn a steady income while waiting for interview calls or acceptance decisions.
- **Show Commitment to Dentistry:** Admissions committees appreciate candidates who actively integrate into the U.S. dental system rather than only focusing on exams.
- **Build Professional Networks:** Working in dental offices opens doors to mentorship, strong recommendation letters, and valuable local connections.
- **Future Licensure Flexibility:** After one year of licensed practice in Florida, you may qualify for hygiene licensure in **Arizona** or **Missouri** without additional coursework.

---

### Requirements for Florida Dental Hygiene Licensure

If you’ve already passed the **INBDE**, you’re partway there. Below are the main steps to qualify for a Florida dental hygiene license.

#### Educational Credentials

- A **dental degree from a non-U.S. dental school**
- Official **credential evaluation** of transcripts through an approved agency such as **ECE** or **WES**

#### Licensing Exams

- **INBDE** – must be successfully passed
- **Florida Laws and Rules Exam** – covers state-specific regulations and ethics
- **ADEX Dental Hygiene Clinical Exam** – a practical, hands-on clinical exam

#### Required Certifications and Documents

- **Current CPR/BLS certification**
- **Background check and fingerprinting**
- **Completed Florida dental hygiene license application** and applicable fees
  - [Florida Dental Hygiene Application](states.florida.dentalHygieneApp)
  - [ADEX Clinical Exam Information](https://adextesting.org/)

</Aside>

## Summary

For many foreign-trained dentists, the **Florida dental hygiene license** is more than an interim job—it’s a smart investment in their U.S. dental career. It offers:

- A **legal and efficient entry** into clinical practice in the U.S.
- A **competitive edge** for Advanced Standing applications through real patient experience
- An opportunity to **gain confidence, income, and local professional connections**

If you’ve already passed the INBDE and are searching for a way to enhance your application, **Florida could be your most practical next step**.
