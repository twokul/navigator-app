The **wax carving procedure** in dental bench tests evaluates your **manual
dexterity, precision, and understanding of dental anatomy**. Schools typically
provide a **tooth model or detailed diagram** for replication, serving as a
reference to ensure anatomical accuracy.

Below is a detailed breakdown of what the procedure entails and what materials schools usually provide.

---

## Detailed Procedure

### Preparation

- You’ll receive a **block of dental wax** and **carving instruments**. Some schools provide the tools; others expect you to bring your own.
- The setup often includes waxing instruments, carving knives, and occasionally a **Bunsen burner** to soften the wax (varies by school).

### Assignment

- You may be asked to carve a **specific tooth or set of teeth**—ranging from a single molar or incisor to multiple structures (crown and root).
- Some schools assign creative shapes (triangle, square, oval, or even an old-school telephone handle).
- Assignments include **morphology instructions** or a **reference image** of the tooth.
- Accurate tooth measurements are essential—use **[Wheeler’s Dental Anatomy (PDF)](/files/wheeler-dental-anatomy.pdf)** as your main reference.  
  Additional resources:
  - [Permanent Teeth – Anatomy Standard](https://www.anatomystandard.com/ossa-et-juncturae/cranium/dentes-permanentes.html)
  - [Concise Dental Anatomy and Morphology](https://www.academia.edu/21433489/Concise_Dental_Anatomy_and_Morphology)

---

## Carving Process

1. **Initial Shaping:** Roughly shape the wax block into the outline of the assigned tooth.
2. **Detailed Carving:** Use fine instruments to define **cusps, ridges, and grooves**.
3. **Refinement:** Smooth surfaces and check proportions against reference diagrams.
4. **Final Touches:** Add fine details and polish for a realistic finish.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Your wax carving will be assessed based on:

- Anatomical **accuracy**
- **Precision** in details
- **Aesthetic quality** and finish
- **Time management** and completion within the allotted time
